Space

After World War II, both the United States and Soviet Union created programs to explore the great unknown. This race spurred unmanned probes primarily till we landed on the Moon landing on July 20, 1969. Since then satellites, telescopes, probes and space facilities have filled the atmosphere around our Earth and other planets in hopes of gathering more information on the great unknown.

Jedi and Wrath of Khan


Since space is so vast, we can only observe and record so many things, because of that directors/authors have decided to allow their imagination take precedence over the science, this imagination lead into some of the greatest perspectives of life in space. Star Trek and Star Wars, respectively, though they take liberties with science of space, do represent life in space and how it would look on semi-realstic scale. These perspectives allow us to live vicariously through the lives of the characters as well as enhance our innate curiousity.
